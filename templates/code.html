iMadeIT.nu

Eagle-eyed viewers may realize that both this site and that site have very similar layouts. I am no web designer!

iMadeIT is a program I ran with for the Northeastern Entrepreneurs Club that teaches students with any level of programming background the fundamentals of web development in Flask by creating a Facebook clone.

To provide a sandbox for students, and add to the "Hey Mom, look what I made!" factor, I created iMadeIT.nu, which, after a user created an account on the website, created an identical account on the server that they could SSH into, and host their web app on our domain.

In addition, the site serves as a one-stop source for information about events, activities, as well as the source code to the site itself, other smaller web-apps, and other useful python resources

For our Capstone project at Northeastern University, my group and I developed a automated trading bot which strives to predict the fluctuations in the cryptocurrency markets

Our bot pulled down market data from various exchanges, as well as posts about the currencies on Reddit.com and then performed a two tiered analysis: first, it regressed the market data over various timeframes to identify the general pattern, and fed the results into a machine-learning algorithm which measured it's own historical accuracy to determine how much confidence it had in the continuation of a given trend. Second, we seperately fed Reddit posts which were flagged as relevant into a NLP suite that determined the sentiment of each post.

We fed the results into a genetic algorithm that spawned traders with the previous analysis and randomly generated weights for their confidence and sentiment. We then simulated trades with these agents, and bred the agents through successive generations until we had the strongest trader of the lot, who became our official live trader (until it was outperformed)

It was a very fun project, and while the group decided to leave the code closed source for now, contact me and I'd be happy to arrange a viewing!
